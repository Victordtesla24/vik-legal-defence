Legal Framework & Doctrine

Primary Authorities

Proudman v Dayman (1941) 67 CLR 536 — HCA
Honest and reasonable mistake of fact is a complete defence to strict-liability offences. Accused must show they genuinely believed they were not driving on a suspended licence and that belief was objectively reasonable given the circumstances (address instability, VicRoads clearance letter, SC D'Cruze communications).
Primary anchor for Charge 1. Cite Dixon CJ at 540.
CTM v The Queen [2008] HCA 25 — 236 CLR 440
Supports strict-liability / mistake analysis for regulatory offences. Confirms Proudman v Dayman doctrine applies broadly to Australian regulatory offences. Cite for evidentiary standard of honest and reasonable belief.
Ref: SR-4
DPP v Kaba [2014] VSC 52
Victoria Supreme Court decision crystallising standards for lawful intercept and reasonable suspicion. Intercepts post-24 Sep 2025 (after VicRoads clearance) may lack lawful basis if predicated solely on LEAP flag that VP itself possessed evidence to contradict (SC D'Cruze email, 13 Oct 2025).
Ref: Comprehensive Legal Strategy Report
Criminal Procedure Act 2009 (Vic) s59 — Diversion
Magistrate may divert matter without conviction if: (a) accused pleads guilty or admits facts; (b) diversion in interests of justice; (c) accused agrees to program/conditions. AGSVA clearance risk, employment impact, zero prior convictions, and civic character make this the highest-yield pathway.
